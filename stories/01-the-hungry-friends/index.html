The Hungry Friends

A silly story about seeds, snacks, and sneaky elephants

🐰 Rabbit 🐘 Elephant
Page 1

Rabbit and Elephant were the very best of friends. One sunny morning, they headed out to their big green field to plant seeds.

Dig, plop, cover. Dig, plop, cover.

The seeds went in one by one — and the hard work was actually quite fun!

Page 2

After a while, Rabbit sat up and patted her tummy. "My tummy is GROWLING!" she said. "I'm SO hungry!"

"Me too!" said Elephant. "Let's order some food!"

Elephant's eyes lit up. "A big fruit salad for me!"

"And CARROT HALWA for me!" cheered Rabbit, hopping with excitement.

Page 3

Elephant pulled out his phone and tapped away. Click, click, click — both dishes ordered in minutes!

"It'll be here soon," said Elephant happily.

"Better wash our hands first!" said Rabbit, and they both trotted off to the old field tap.

Page 4

Elephant went first — he scrubbed, splashed, and hummed a little song. Rabbit was still washing when the delivery arrived.

Elephant walked over to pick up both parcels. He peeked inside his fruit salad. "Mmm!"

Then he peeked at Rabbit's Carrot Halwa. Golden. Warm. Sweet-smelling. And just like that… the whole bowl was gone.

Page 5

Rabbit came running back, hands still a little damp. She grabbed her parcel, tore it open, and looked inside.

Empty.

Completely, entirely, totally EMPTY.

"What… happened to my Carrot Halwa?" she asked, blinking at the hollow bowl.

Page 6

"No idea!" said Elephant, with his most innocent smile.

Rabbit stared. And stared. And stared some more.

Elephant's ears began to droop. His trunk began to fidget.

Finally, he said quietly: "It smelled so amazing… I ate the whole thing. I'm really, truly sorry."

"But I was SO looking forward to it!" said Rabbit sadly.

Page 7

Elephant pushed his big fruit bowl across with his trunk. "Please share mine. I truly am sorry."

Rabbit let out a long, long sigh.

Then smiled — just a tiny smile.

They shared the fruit bowl, bite by bite, side by side under the shady tree. Then headed right back to planting seeds.

Page 8

The very next morning, Rabbit's eyes twinkled as they walked to the field.

"Elephant!" she said. "That fruit salad yesterday was actually really good! Can we order the same today? One for you, one small one for me?"

Elephant grinned and placed the order on his phone right away.

Page 9

But then — Elephant remembered something. He had packed his own lunch today! A big stack of golden pancakes sat in his bag.

A slow, sneaky grin spread across his face.

While Rabbit was at the tap, Elephant ate her fruit bowl… and quietly tucked his pancakes into her empty container. 😏

Page 10

Rabbit bounced back and tore open her box excitedly. She stopped. Blinked. Tilted her head sideways.

Pancakes.

"But I ordered a fruit bowl!" she said slowly, looking up at Elephant.

"How did pancakes get in here?"

Page 11

Elephant explained everything — the lunch from home, the irresistible fruit bowl, the sneaky pancake swap.

"And besides," he added, "I don't even like pancakes that much!"

Rabbit threw her paws straight up in the air.

"AGAIN, Elephant?!"

There was a lot of sighing. A lot of trunk-shrugging. But finally — an agreement.

Page 12

Half pancake and half fruit bowl — each!

They sat under their favourite shady tree, eating every last bite. Then, full and perfectly happy, they both dozed off for a little nap — snoring softly in the afternoon sun.

When they woke up, they looked at each other and burst out laughing. Then, side by side, they went right back to planting seeds.

Just like always. ✨ The End ✨
