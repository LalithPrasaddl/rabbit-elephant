Snow Much Help!

A story about kindness, mistakes, and second chances

🐰 Rabbit 🐘 Elephant 🐿️ Dr. Squirrel 🐑 Dr. Sheep 🦒 Nurse Giraffe
Page 1

Deep in the forest, snow began to fall — softly at first, then thicker and thicker, until the whole forest was blanketed in white.

All the animals rushed outside to play! They built snowmen, threw snowballs, and slid down icy hills — without a single scarf or mitten between them.

Page 2

Rabbit and Elephant, however, stayed inside. They had both just come home from the hospital after foot surgery, and their toes were still healing.

So while everyone else played in the snow, Rabbit and Elephant curled up by the fire with big bowls of warm soup — safe, cosy, and very content.

Page 3

But the snow kept falling — for days and days. Soon, animal after animal came down with coughs, colds, and chills from playing out in the cold without warm clothes.

The hospital filled up fast. Too fast. It sent an urgent message out to the whole forest: "We need healthy helpers — please, anyone who can, come lend a hand!"

Page 4

Rabbit and Elephant looked at each other. Their toes were healed now, and they knew exactly what to do.

At the hospital, Dr. Squirrel greeted them with a grateful smile. "Elephant, please help Nurse Giraffe on the ward. Rabbit, please help fetch food and supplies for our patients."

And so, their very first day of helping began.

Page 5

Nurse Giraffe brought Elephant to Pig's bedside. "Pig needs a breathing mask," she said, and hurried off to fetch supplies.

Elephant looked at the shelf of masks and picked the biggest one — his own size! — and popped it right onto Pig's snout.

Then he turned to chat with Nurse Giraffe, not noticing the machine humming to life behind him…

Page 6

The oversized mask blasted a huge gust of air — and Pig went flying clean off the bed, landing against the wall with a loud THUD!

Elephant spun around. "Oh no — sorry, Pig!" He scooped Pig up gently, tucked him back into bed, and — after checking carefully with Nurse Giraffe — fitted him with the right, Pig-sized mask.

Elephant's first day was off to a rather rocky start.

Page 7

Across the hall, Rabbit had her own assignment. Deer had a cold and needed warm chilli soup. Fox had a fever and needed cooling ice cream.

Rabbit dashed off, arms full of both — but in her hurry, she mixed them up completely: icy-cold ice cream for shivering Deer, and fiery hot chilli soup for feverish Fox!

Page 8

Poor Deer shivered even harder with each cold spoonful, while Fox turned redder and redder with every spicy sip. Both patients felt much, much worse.

Dr. Sheep came rushing in at once. "Oh dear — wrong dishes!" She swapped the bowls right away, tucked a blanket around Deer, and fanned Fox with a leaf until both patients settled back down.

Page 9

Elephant was sent to help in one more room, where a giraffe patient with a fever needed an injection, and Wolf with a cold needed tablets.

But Elephant mixed them up too — he gave Wolf's cold tablets to the giraffe, and grabbed the big giraffe-sized injection meant for the fever… and gave it straight to Wolf!

Wolf let out a howl so loud the whole hospital heard it.

Page 10

Dr. Sheep called Rabbit and Elephant into her office. She thanked them kindly for trying so hard — but gently suggested that maybe, today, they should just go home.

Rabbit and Elephant looked at each other, feeling very sad. "Please," said Elephant, "just one more chance?"

Dr. Sheep thought for a moment, then smiled. "One more chance. Let's see how you do."

Page 11

This time, Elephant was sent to Lion's room to change his bandage. He worked slowly and carefully — unwrapping the old bandage, gently cleaning the wound, and wrapping on a fresh, neat one.

Lion gave a satisfied nod. Elephant had gotten it exactly right.

Page 12

Meanwhile, Rabbit was asked to drive old Tortoise — who had just had back surgery — safely home. She packed up all of Tortoise's things, helped him gently into the back of the truck, and drove slowly and carefully through the snowy roads.

She dropped Tortoise safely at his front door, then drove straight back to the hospital to see if anyone else needed help.

Page 13

For the rest of the day, there wasn't a single mishap. At the end of it all, Dr. Sheep gathered Rabbit and Elephant together. "Thank you both, truly," she said, handing them each a chocolate. "Same time tomorrow?"

Rabbit and Elephant grinned from ear to ear (and ear to trunk). All the way home, they chattered happily about their busy day — already looking forward to coming back and helping again. ✨ The End ✨
