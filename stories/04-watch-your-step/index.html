Watch Your Step!

A story about thorns, teamwork, and paying attention

🐰 Rabbit 🐘 Elephant 🐑 Dr. Sheep
Page 1

One morning, Rabbit and Elephant were out in the field, planting seeds for their garden.

"Noodles for lunch today?" asked Rabbit. "Or maybe... samosas?" wondered Elephant, turning to look at Rabbit instead of watching where he stepped.

Dig, plop, cover — and a whole lot of talking about food.

Page 2

Elephant was so busy staring at Rabbit's face, wondering about lunch, that he didn't look down at all.

CRUNCH. His big foot came down right on a giant thorn — one with roots that went deep, deep into the ground.

"OWWW!" Elephant bellowed so loudly that birds flew straight out of the trees.

Page 3

Elephant tried to lift his foot. He pulled. He wiggled. He grunted.

Nothing happened. The thorn's roots held on tight, and Elephant could only sit right where he was, leg throbbing.

"I can't move," he said miserably. "Not even a little bit."

Page 4

Rabbit didn't know what to do. She hopped in a circle three times, then remembered — Dr. Sheep!

She dashed to the old telephone by the fence and turned the crank as fast as her paws could go.

"Dr. Sheep! Elephant's stuck! Please come quick!"

Page 5

Dr. Sheep arrived and knelt down beside Elephant's foot. She frowned thoughtfully.

"This thorn's roots go far too deep to just pull out," she said. "We're going to need a JCB."

"A what-now?" asked Rabbit. "A big digging machine," said Dr. Sheep. "Trust me."

Page 6

The JCB rumbled into the field, its big yellow arm swinging.

Scoop! Scoop! Scoop! It dug careful holes all around Elephant's leg, right down to where the thorn's roots twisted through the dirt.

Snip — the digger's bucket sliced clean through the roots at last.

Page 7

Elephant's leg came free — hooray! But a little piece of thorn was still stuck deep in his foot.

There was no time to walk all the way to the hospital, so Elephant simply climbed into the JCB's big bucket, and off they trundled down the road.

It was, Elephant admitted, a rather bumpy but exciting ride.

Page 8

At the hospital, Dr. Sheep worked with tiny tweezers, gently and carefully, until the very last piece of thorn came out.

"All done," she said, patting Elephant's trunk. "But you'll need to rest right here for one whole week. No planting, no walking — just resting."

Elephant sighed, but he knew Dr. Sheep was right.

Page 9

With Elephant resting, Rabbit went back to the field to keep planting — but it wasn't nearly as fun alone.

She kept thinking about her friend in his hospital bed, wondering if he was bored, wondering what he'd say next.

She was thinking so hard about Elephant that she completely forgot to watch her own feet.

Page 10

CRUNCH. Rabbit's foot landed right on a thorn of her very own — smaller than Elephant's, but it still hurt plenty.

"Ow, ow, ow!" Rabbit hopped on one foot all the way to the telephone. "Dr. Sheep, it happened to ME this time!"

"On my way," said Dr. Sheep, already reaching for her car keys.

Page 11

Rabbit gulped. "Should I call the JCB too?"

Dr. Sheep laughed kindly. "Elephant is enormous — he needed a whole digger. But you? You're little. I can manage just fine with my own two hooves."

She slipped the small thorn out in seconds, then drove Rabbit to the hospital in her car, stitched up the tiny wound, and tucked her into the bed right next to Elephant's.

Page 12

So there they were — two best friends, side by side in the same hospital room, resting for a whole week together.

When they finally returned to the field, they planted seeds just like before. But this time, every single step, both of them looked exactly where they were putting their feet.

They still talked and laughed, just a little less, and a lot more carefully. ✨ The End ✨
