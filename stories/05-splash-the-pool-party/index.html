Splash! The Pool Party

A story about respecting space, saying sorry, and making new friends

🐰 Rabbit 🐘 Elephant
Page 1

A big sign appeared at the forest resort: "POOL PARTY TODAY! All animals welcome!"

Elephant zoomed up in his little car, tooting the horn happily. Rabbit hopped off a bouncy bus, her ears flapping in the wind.

They had never met before. But today, that was about to change!

Page 2

After a yummy breakfast of berries and buns, Rabbit couldn't wait one more second.

She hopped straight into the big, sparkling pool and paddled around all by herself, giggling at her own splashes.

"This pool is all mine today!" she laughed.

Page 3

Elephant finished his breakfast too — and he was SO excited about swimming that he ran full speed toward the pool and jumped!

SPLOOSH! A humongous wave lifted Rabbit clean out of the water and popped her high into the sky.

Down she came again with a great big plop.

Page 4

Rabbit paddled over, dripping and grumpy. "That splash was way too big!" she said.

Elephant looked down at her. "This pool is for big animals like me," he said kindly. "There's a smaller pool right over there, just for little ones."

But Rabbit crossed her arms and kept swimming right where she was.

Page 5

Still excited to play, Elephant jumped in again. SPLASH!

Up went Rabbit, even higher this time, and down she came with an even bigger plop.

"Okay, okay — I get it now!" she sighed. It really was time to try somewhere else.

Page 6

Rabbit hopped over to the small pool and met Squirrel and a whole gang of little friends, splashing and laughing together.

Meanwhile, over in the big pool, Elephant made enormous waves with Giraffe and the other big animals.

Both pools were full of fun — just the right size for everyone in them.

Page 7

When lunchtime came, Rabbit and Elephant bumped into each other in line.

"I'm sorry I didn't listen when you tried to tell me," said Rabbit. "And I'm sorry I wasn't more careful with my splashes," said Elephant.

They both smiled. Saying sorry really did make things better.

Page 8

They sat down and shared lunch together, swapping stories and laughing so hard Rabbit nearly fell off her chair.

Afterward, they played tennis, and then basketball, cheering each other on with every point.

By the end of the day, they'd had the best time ever.

Page 9

As the sun began to set, Elephant asked, "Where do you live? I could give you a ride home."

Rabbit hopped happily into his car, and he dropped her right at her burrow door.

From that day on, Rabbit and Elephant were the very best of friends. ✨ The End ✨
